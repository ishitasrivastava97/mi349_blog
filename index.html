Rajma Masala

Ishita Srivastava
Oct 6, 2020

Welcome to Festive Flavors! Today we are going to cook Rajma Masala, a dish made of "Rajma" beans or Red Kidney Beans. Rajma is an extremely healthy legume which one should definitely add to their diet, and what's a tastier way to cook this legume than Rajma Masala. It is a staple of every North Indian household and the best way to serve is with rice- the popular Indian Rajma Chawal.

Let's look at the ingredients:
  • Rajma - 1 cup uncooked
  • Onion - 1 sliced
  • Tomato paste - 1 cup
  • Garlic paste - 1 tbsp
  • Ginger paste- 2 tsp
  • Green chili as per taste
  • Bay leaves - 2
  • Black peppers- 1/2 tsp
  • Cinnamon - 1/2 inch piece
  • Black Cardamom - 1
  • Coriander powder- 1 tbsp
  • Turmeric powder - 1 tsp
  • Garam masala - 2 tsp
  • Oil - 2 tbsp
  • Salt to taste
Let's get started:
  1. First, presoak the Rajma beans overnight.
  2. In a pressure cooker add beans, water and salt and pressure cook for 2 whistles and then on low heat for 10 minutes. Let the pressure realse on it's own and then open the cooker.
  3. Strain the liquid of the beans and keep it aside for the gravy.
  4. Heat a pan, add oil. Now add Cumin seeds, black pepper, Cinnamon, Black Cardamom and Bay leaves.
  5. Add onions and fry till golden brown.
  6. Now add the garlic paste and ginger paste. Add a table spoon of water. Fry for 2 to 3 minutes.
  7. Add Turmeric powder and coriander powder. Fry for 2 to 3 minutes.
  8. Now add salt. Close the lid for a 1 to 2 minutes to cook the Masala.
  9. add the Tomato paste and stir. Tomato paste is just ground chopped tomato into a paste.
  10. Add the green chili paste and stir again.
  11. Cover and cook for 3-4 minutes or until the Masala leaves the oil.
  12. Add the boiled and strained Rajma in the Masala and stir.
  13. Add Garam Masala to this and mix well.
  14. Add the Rajma water that was kept aside while straining Boiled Rajma.
  15. Mix everything, cover, and bring to a boil. Now lower the heat and cook for 5 to 7 minutes so that the Masala imparts a flavor to the Rajma beans.
  16. The Rajma looks done now. Discard the Bay leaves. Garnish it with chopped coriander leaves.

Rajma Masala is ready to be served. You can serve it with Roti or Naan and rice. A very popular serving suggestion is to serve it with just plain rice. Enjoy the filling comfort food. For a more detailed explanation, please visit Festive Flavors' youtube channel.

Fish Curry

Ishita Srivastava
Oct 6, 2020

Today we are cooking the delectable North Indian UP style Fish Curry. The secret to this curry is Fenugreek seeds which impart a distinct bittersweet and earthy flavor. Another important component to this recipe is Mustard Oil. Always use Mustard oil for this recipe. You'll love to make this recipe again and again. Fish, as you know, are very healthy as they have numerous health benefits and plus they are light on the tummy.

Let's look at the ingredients:
Item Amount Spice Level
Rohu Fish/Catfish 1.5 kg N/A
Onion 1 sliced N/A
Tomato 1 chopped N/A
Garlic Paste 1 tbsp N/A
Giner Paste 1 tsp N/A
Green chilli 13 pieces High
Turmeric Powder 1 tsp N/A
Coriander Powder 1.5 tbsp N/A
Kashmiri Chilli Powder 1 tsp Mild
Garam Masala 1 tsp Medium
Bay Leaves 2 pieces N/A
Fenugreek Seeds 1 tsp N/A
Salt Preference N/A
Kasoori Methi 1 tbsp N/A
Mustard Oil 1/4 cups N/A
Coriander Leaves 1/4 tbsp N/A
Water 500 mL N/A
Here are some tips and tricks to keep in mind before getting started:
  1. Clean the fish very well.
  2. Cook the garlic ginger paste really well to remove the raw flavor of garlic.
  3. Add Fenugreek seeds. This imparts a great flavor to this UP style Fish curry.
  4. Turn the fish in the Masala in the initial part of cooking but then turn carefully once the fish is half cooked so that it does not break.
  5. Fish cooks really quickly.
Let's get started:
  1. Clean and wash the fish steaks. Drain all the water and keep it aside.
  2. Heat Mustard Oil in a pan. It's best to use Mustard oil for making UP style Fish Curry.
  3. Add Fenugreek seeds and, Bay leaves.
  4. Add Sliced Onions. Cook them till golden in color.
  5. Add Garlic paste and gingeri paste. Add a spoonful of water.
  6. Add Turmeric Powder, Coriander Powder and Kashmiri red chili powder.
  7. Add some water if the masala dries up quickly. Cook till the raw smell of Garlic goes away.
  8. Cover and cook for 2 minutes.
  9. Add chopped Tomatoes.
  10. Add green chilies and salt.
  11. Crush half of the Kasoori methi leaves and add in the Masala. This gives a great flavor to the Fish curry.
  12. Mix everything and cover and cook till the tomatoes become mushy.
  13. Mash the Tomatoes if they are still not pulpy.
  14. Add the Fish pieces to the Masala.
  15. After 2 minutes turn the Fish pieces and mix with the Masala.
  16. Keep turning and cooking till the Fish pieces become whitish in color.
  17. Mix everything. But do not turn a lot as the fish might break.
  18. Cover and cook for 10 minutes by turning one or two times.
  19. Now crush and add leftover Kasoori Methi leaves. Add Garam Masala and green coriander leaves. Mix everything. The yummylicious and lip smacking Fish curry is ready to serve with rice.
